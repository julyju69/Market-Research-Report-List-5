<p><p>The coke for electrode market encompasses various types, each serving specific industrial needs. Petroleum coke is derived from oil refining and used in aluminum production, while pitch coke is produced from carbonizing petroleum pitch, mainly for electrodes in electric arc furnaces. Metallurgical coke, made from coal, is essential in steel manufacturing. Needle coke, known for its high electrical conductivity, is crucial for premium electrodes. Other types of coke include sponge and foundry coke, catering to diverse applications in various industries.</p></p>
