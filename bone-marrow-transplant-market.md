<p><p>The bone marrow transplant market is primarily categorized into two types: autologous and allogeneic transplants. </p><p>Autologous bone marrow transplant involves harvesting the patient’s own stem cells, which are then reinfused after intensive treatment, reducing the risk of rejection. </p><p>In contrast, allogeneic bone marrow transplant uses stem cells from a donor, either related or unrelated, which can provide a better match for certain conditions but carries a higher risk of complications, such as graft-versus-host disease.</p></p>
