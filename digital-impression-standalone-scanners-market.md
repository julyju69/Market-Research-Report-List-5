<p><p>Digital impression standalone scanners are specialized devices used in dentistry to capture precise 3D images of patients' teeth and gums. Two primary types are confocal microscopic imaging scanners and optical wand scanners. Confocal microscopic imaging scanners utilize laser technology to produce high-resolution images by focusing on specific points within a sample, allowing for detailed examination. Optical wand scanners, on the other hand, employ a handheld wand to quickly capture digital impressions by taking multiple images, providing a smooth and efficient scanning experience.</p></p>
