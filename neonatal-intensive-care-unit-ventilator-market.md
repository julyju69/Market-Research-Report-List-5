<p><p>The Neonatal Intensive Care Unit (NICU) ventilator market comprises two main types: invasive and non-invasive ventilators. Invasive ventilators are utilized for newborns requiring direct airway support, often through intubation, providing precise control over respiratory parameters. In contrast, non-invasive ventilators deliver respiratory assistance through interfaces like masks, reducing the risk of complications associated with intubation. Both types play crucial roles in managing respiratory distress in neonates, with their choice depending on the severity of the infant's condition and overall medical strategy.</p></p>
