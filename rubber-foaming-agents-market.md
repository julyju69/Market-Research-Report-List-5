<p><p>The rubber foaming agents market is primarily divided into two types: inorganic and organic foaming agents. Inorganic foaming agents, such as sodium bicarbonate, rely on chemical reactions to produce gas, offering heat resistance and stability. Organic foaming agents, including azodicarbonamide and blowing agents, generate gas at elevated temperatures, providing lightweight rubber products with enhanced flexibility. Both types serve crucial roles in determining the physical properties of rubber, thereby impacting applications across automotive, footwear, and industrial sectors.</p></p>
