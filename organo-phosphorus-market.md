<p><ul><li>Three Coordinated Phosphorus Compound</li><li>Four Coordinated Phosphorus Compound</li><li>Five Coordinated Phosphorus Compound</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Organo-phosphorus compounds are classified based on the number of coordinated phosphorus atoms in their structure. Three coordinated phosphorus compounds have three bonds to phosphorus, forming trigonal planar geometry. Four coordinated phosphorus compounds have four bonds, forming a tetrahedral geometry. Five coordinated phosphorus compounds have five bonds, forming a trigonal bipyramidal geometry. Each type of compound has unique properties and applications in various industries such as agriculture, pharmaceuticals, and materials science.</p></p>
