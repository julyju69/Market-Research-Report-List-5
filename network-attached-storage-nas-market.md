<p><p>Network-Attached Storage (NAS) is a file storage solution that connects to a network, allowing multiple users and devices to access and share data. The market for NAS can be categorized into two main types: scale-up and scale-out. Scale-up NAS focuses on enhancing capacity and performance by adding more disks to existing systems, making it ideal for organizations with predictable growth. In contrast, scale-out NAS enables seamless addition of multiple devices, optimizing performance and capacity, suitable for dynamic workloads and extensive data needs.</p></p>
