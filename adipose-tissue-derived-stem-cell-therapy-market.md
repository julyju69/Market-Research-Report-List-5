<p><p>Adipose tissue-derived stem cell therapy market encompasses two primary types: autologous and allogeneic stem cells. Autologous stem cells are harvested from a patient's own adipose tissue, minimizing the risk of immune rejection and complications. In contrast, allogeneic stem cells are sourced from donors, allowing for potential scalability and use in patients who may not have adequate personal reserves. Both types hold promise for regenerative medicine, particularly in treating orthopedic conditions, cardiovascular diseases, and cosmetic applications.</p></p>
