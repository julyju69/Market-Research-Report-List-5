<p><ul><li>Complementary Metal Oxide Semiconductor (CMOS)</li><li>Charge-Coupled Device (CCD)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive camera sensor market primarily consists of two types: Complementary Metal Oxide Semiconductor (CMOS) and Charge-Coupled Device (CCD) sensors. CMOS sensors are favored for their low power consumption, faster processing speeds, and integration capabilities, making them ideal for advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) and autonomous vehicles. On the other hand, CCD sensors are known for their high image quality and sensitivity, which makes them suitable for applications requiring superior imaging performance, such as high-resolution rear-view cameras.</p></p>
