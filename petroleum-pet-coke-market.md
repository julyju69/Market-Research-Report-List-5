<p><p>The petroleum (PET) coke market is primarily divided into two types: calcined coke and fuel-grade coke. Calcined coke is produced by heating green petroleum coke to remove volatile substances, resulting in a high-purity product mainly used in aluminum production and as a carbon source in various industries. Fuel-grade coke, on the other hand, contains higher impurities and is primarily utilized as an alternative fuel source in power generation and cement manufacturing. Both types cater to distinct industrial needs and applications.</p></p>
