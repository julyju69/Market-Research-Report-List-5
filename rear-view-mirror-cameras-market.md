<p><p>The rear view mirror cameras market primarily features two types of imaging technologies: CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) and CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) cameras. CCD cameras are known for their superior image quality and low-light performance, making them ideal for capturing detailed visuals during night driving. In contrast, CMOS cameras tend to be more cost-effective, consume less power, and offer faster processing speeds, making them popular for various vehicle applications. Each type caters to different consumer preferences and vehicle technologies.</p></p>
