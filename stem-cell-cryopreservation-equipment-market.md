<p><ul><li>Liquid Phase</li><li>Vapor Phase</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Stem Cell Cryopreservation Equipment encompasses two primary market types: liquid phase and vapor phase. Liquid phase cryopreservation involves storing stem cells directly in liquid nitrogen, providing a stable temperature and consistent conditions for long-term preservation. In contrast, vapor phase cryopreservation utilizes the gaseous nitrogen above the liquid, allowing for a gradual temperature decrease and minimizing risks of thermal shock. Both methods are essential for maintaining stem cell viability, enabling effective research and therapeutic applications in regenerative medicine.</p></p>
