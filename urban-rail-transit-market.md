<p><p>Urban rail transit encompasses various systems designed to facilitate efficient urban mobility. Metro rail operates on dedicated tracks, typically underground or elevated, providing high-capacity service with frequent stops. Light rail systems, often at ground level, feature a lighter design and cater to shorter distances with flexible routes. Monorail systems utilize a single track, offering unique solutions in constrained environments. Each type serves distinct urban needs, enhancing connectivity while navigating space limitations and passenger demand in densely populated areas.</p></p>
