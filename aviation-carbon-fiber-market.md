<p><p>The aviation carbon fiber market comprises two primary types: continuous fiber and chopped fiber. Continuous fiber consists of long strands that provide superior strength and rigidity, making it ideal for high-performance components in aircraft structures. Chopped fiber, on the other hand, is made of shorter strands and is typically used in applications requiring less structural integrity, such as composite materials and non-structural parts. Both types cater to varying aerospace needs, driven by demand for lightweight and durable materials.</p></p>
