<p><p>Automotive Adaptive Cruise Control (ACC) systems utilize different technologies such as radar, LIDAR, and sensor fusion to enhance vehicle safety and convenience. Radar-based systems use radio waves to detect objects in front of the vehicle, while LIDAR systems use laser beams for accurate distance measurement. Sensor fusion combines data from multiple sensors like cameras, radars, and LIDARs to provide comprehensive situational awareness. Each technology has its own advantages and limitations, but when combined, they offer a more reliable and effective ACC system for vehicles.</p></p>
