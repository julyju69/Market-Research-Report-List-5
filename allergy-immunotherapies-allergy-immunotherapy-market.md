<p><p>Allergy immunotherapy involves treatments aimed at desensitizing individuals to specific allergens, helping to reduce symptoms over time. There are two main types: subcutaneous immunotherapy (SCIT), which involves injections of allergens administered at regular intervals, typically in a clinical setting, to build tolerance; and sublingual immunotherapy (SLIT), where allergens are placed under the tongue, allowing for home administration and a more convenient experience. Both approaches aim to provide long-term relief and improve the quality of life for allergy sufferers.</p></p>
