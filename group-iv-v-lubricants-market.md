<p><p>The Group IV lubricants, primarily polyalphaolefins (PAOs), are synthetic oils known for high stability, viscosity, and resistance to oxidation, making them suitable for high-performance applications. Group V lubricants encompass various synthetic bases, including esters and polyalkylene glycols (PAGs), which offer biodegradability and excellent lubricity. Esters provide optimal thermal stability and are often used in environmentally friendly formulations, while PAGs excel in water-soluble applications, delivering superior lubrication and seal compatibility. Together, these groups cater to diverse industrial needs.</p></p>
