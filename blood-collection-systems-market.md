<p><p>The blood collection systems market is categorized into several types. Serum separating tubes are used for obtaining serum by allowing blood to clot and then separating the liquid component via centrifugation. EDTA tubes contain anticoagulants for preserving blood samples for hematological analyses. Plasma separation tubes facilitate the collection of plasma after centrifugation. Other types include tubes with additives for specialized tests, such as those for glucose or hormone levels, catering to diverse diagnostic needs in healthcare settings.</p></p>

<p><p>The blood collection systems market encompasses technologies and devices used for the efficient collection of blood samples from patients. Venous blood collection involves drawing blood from veins, typically for diagnostic testing, requiring specialized equipment like needles and vacuum tubes. In contrast, capillary blood collection involves obtaining blood via small punctures, often from fingertip or heel pricks, making it more suitable for home testing and pediatric use. Both methods are crucial for patient diagnostics and monitoring in various healthcare settings.</p></p>
